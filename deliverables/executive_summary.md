# Executive Summary, Can Ads or Sponsorship Fund Free Care at Legion?

**One page. Full analysis: deck.html · growth_contribution_analysis.md · data/uninsured_population_sizing.md · model/legion_sponsorship_model.xlsx · assumptions_summary.md**

## What Legion should do

Do **not** replace Legion's reimbursed FFS model. **None of ≈3,000 actives today are uninsured** — this analysis scopes ads/sponsorship to a **new incremental uninsured pipeline**. National TAM: ≈5.9M uninsured adults with mental illness (Census/NSDUH). Run ≈$10–25K validation **before** building that pipeline: sponsor WTP interviews + patient trust survey. Legion's operating data is integrated into the model. Survey: https://legion-take-home-assignment.vercel.app/

## What Legion should not do

Build a patient-facing ad platform as a growth strategy toward $1B (ads contribute &lt;0.1% of revenue even at ≈50K actives). Treat sponsorship as the valuation story (at scale: ≈0.2–1.5% of a $100M company at realistic WTP). Accept diagnosis-level targeting, patient-level data sharing, or prescription-linked economics; accept capitated risk while costs remain directional.

## Why

* **Uninsured TAM (Census/NSDUH 2024, P01–P08):** ≈27.5M uninsured → ≈5.9M with AMI → ≈1.5M in treatment; A13 base 150K = reachable capture. For uninsured, sponsor bar is **COGS** (≈$8–$74), not $153 reimbursement.
* **Unit economics (today):** Ads ≈$1.53/patient/yr (≈2% of ≈$79 break-even on AI pathway). Sponsorship ≈$10.50 net (≈13%, ≈7x gap as primary funder). CEO costs: $74/visit → $37 leveraged, ≈$1/AI renewal, 5.3 visits/patient, CAC $250, mature LTV ≈$2,025 booked (8.1x).
* **Growth lens (eventual $1B = ≈$100M/yr over years, not six months):** Core FFS scales linearly with actives. At ≈50K actives (≈$99M core), sponsorship on 15K free-pathway patients contributes ≈$158K (base) to ≈$1.5M (if $100/patient WTP trigger fires) = **0.2–1.5%** of company revenue. Ads: ≈$23K (&lt;0.1%). Employer underwriting on 50K covered lives: ≈$16.5M (≈17%). **The $1B path is core growth + margin expansion (76% GM) + optional underwriting — not ads/sponsorship.**
* **Free AI as CAC:** ≈$8/episode vs $250 CAC → ≈3.2% free-to-paid conversion breaks even; feeds the core engine as Legion grows, with or without sponsors.
* **Regulatory/evidence:** BetterHelp/GoodRx enforcement; Lotus "sponsorships" unverified; OpenEvidence physician CPMs don't transfer to patients.
* **Cheap test scope:** Sponsor WTP interviews + patient trust survey test the **literal ads/sponsorship question** before scale. No ad pilot (gap ≈50x at base; immaterial contribution even at 50K actives). Underwriting interviews run in parallel.

## What remains unknown

Contractable PEPM for a narrow AI psychiatry benefit (#1 sensitivity) and realistic utilization (#2); patient trust impact including spillover to Legion's paying book; whether any non pharma sponsor category shows real willingness to pay; audited versions of Legion's cost inputs. New question the data unlocked: **free AI care as an acquisition channel**. A free AI episode costs ≈$8 to deliver vs. ≈$250 paid CAC, so a ≈3% free to paid conversion rate breaks even; conversion is testable in the same pilot.

## The test that resolves it

Legion's operating data is integrated. Next: counsel threshold read; 15–20 buyer interviews; randomized 4-arm patient concept test (live survey above). Days 27–30: proceed / modify / stop decision.

## What success looks like

Economics survived the data gate (done); median qualified buyer WTP above the model threshold with ≥1 paid design partner and ≥2 prospects advancing; patient test within guardrails (≤5pp continuation decline, ≤0.25 trust decline, ≥80% disclosure comprehension); counsel clears the structure with zero patient data flows.

## What makes Legion stop

Buyer WTP clusters at the EAP floor (≈$36/patient/yr); any funder demands patient data or clinical influence; trust guardrails breach; economics only work through steering; or reimbursement expansion is clearly the better use of founder time. One trigger already fired: episode cost came in well below the ≈$75 threshold I set before receiving Legion's data (≈$8), so sponsorship is back on the table as a potential co-funder — pending WTP evidence from buyer interviews.
