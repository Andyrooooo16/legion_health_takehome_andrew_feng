# Why a Funder Pays Legion: The Value Proposition

**One page. Answers the uncomfortable question: why pay for access to roughly 3,000 patients?** Companion sources: `sponsor_test_package.md` §1 · `research/comparable_notes.md` (Decision Implications) · strategic completeness audit gap 2.

## What Legion is NOT selling

**Reach.** Legion has roughly 3,000 active patients today. That is not a media audience. Conventional health media (WebMD-scale inventory) sells impressions; Legion cannot and should not compete there.

**Targeting.** Funders receive zero patient-level data, ever. Placement is contextual only (same credit shown to every patient in a pathway), by hard constraint. Diagnosis-level or behavioral targeting is excluded, not a negotiation point.

## What Legion IS selling

1. **Premium association** with a trusted psychiatric-care brand at the moment care is granted, not adjacent to a content article.
2. **Category exclusivity** (scarcity: one funder per category per pathway per period), a priced term packages A/B already contemplate.
3. **Underwritten access:** the funder visibly makes free care exist for a defined cohort, with aggregate outcome reporting (cohort-level activation, completion, satisfaction; never individual data). This is Package A's core offer.
4. **A growth option:** early partners lock exclusivity before the AI pathway scales, so the first deals buy position, not just today's inventory.

## Why Legion over conventional health media

Patient-health-vertical CPMs of roughly $20-45 sell reach at scale (`comparable_notes.md`). Legion sells something those inventories cannot: trust context (a care experience, not a content site), an outcomes story no banner ad can offer, and exclusivity that WebMD-scale inventory cannot provide. The pitch is association and access, not cost-per-thousand.

## Minimum-scale hypothesis (testable)

We believe premium association and access, not reach, is the product, so a high-intent cohort of 1,000-5,000 eligible patients is sufficient inventory for a fee-for-benefit or access-underwriting deal.

**KILL CONDITION:** if qualified buyers consistently require more than ~25,000 exposed patients before engaging, this value proposition fails and only the employer PEPM route (where the buyer funds its own population) remains.

## Reporting Legion can safely provide

Aggregate exposure; aggregate engagement with sponsored modules; cohort-level care-completion and satisfaction stats; opt-out rates. Never: individual identities, diagnoses, prescriptions, or behavioral profiles.
